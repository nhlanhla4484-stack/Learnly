🌊 Part 1 of 3 — Evaporation

Step 1: Water becomes vapour

The sun heats water in oceans, rivers, and lakes. When water gets warm enough, it turns into an invisible gas called water vapour and rises into the air.

This process is called evaporation. You can see it when a puddle disappears on a sunny day — the water didn't vanish, it evaporated!

☁️ Part 2 of 3 — Condensation & Precipitation

Steps 2 & 3: Clouds form, then it rains!

As water vapour rises high into the sky, it cools down. Cold air can't hold much water vapour, so it turns back into tiny water droplets. These droplets cluster together to form clouds. This is called condensation.

When clouds collect enough water droplets, they get heavy. The water falls back to earth as rain, snow, or hail. This is called precipitation.

💧 rises → cools → ☁️ forms (condensation) → gets heavy → 🌧️ falls (precipitation)
