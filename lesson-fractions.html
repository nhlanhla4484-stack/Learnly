📖 Part 1 of 3 — What is a fraction?

A fraction is a piece of something whole

Imagine you have a pizza. If you cut it into 2 equal pieces and eat one piece, you ate ½ (one half) of the pizza.

A fraction has two numbers:

½ The top number (numerator) = how many pieces you have
The bottom number (denominator) = total equal pieces
2 equal pieces
½ eaten
4 equal pieces
¾ eaten

The key rule: the pieces must always be equal!

📖 Part 2 of 3 — Fractions of a group

Fractions work with groups too!

Fractions aren't just for food — they work for groups of things too.

Imagine you have 8 apples. You give 4 away. You gave away 4/8 — which is the same as ½!

🍎🍎🍎🍎 | 🍎🍎🍎🍎 4 given away out of 8 total = 4/8 = ½

When we simplify 4/8: 4÷4=1 and 8÷4=2, so 4/8 = ½. This is called simplifying.

Which fraction is bigger?
